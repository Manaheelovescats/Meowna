    • Survival Instincts

    Of course, cats are good hunters and have their natural instincts. These instincts are very useful and are often used in their lives.

    1. The most behavioral instinct they have is their hunting instinct. This aids them with food.
    2. Their possession of reflex action. These instincts help them survive outdoors.
    3. Their ability to be extremely skilled at climbing.
    4. They have high hearing and a strong sense of smell , which help them throughout their hunts and scavenges and to navigate their locations.
    5. Their natural ability to find shelter , is another Instinct that helps with their outdoor survival.

    Specific Survival Instincts of Wild Cats

    Cats are brave creatures. They are good at thriving for themselves and are careful at all times.
    Their way of survival is well done, and here are some instincts of stray or wild cats.

    1. Although they already have a hunting instinct, wild cats are extremely good at hunting and have strong hunting instincts.
    2. They enjoy stalking and killing their prey.
    3. They mark their scents or engage in scent marking more.
    4. They have a more specific diet to follow, which is hard to maintain in householdsâ€” making them more different from domestic cats.
    5. They are also highly territorial as they are more competitive and will do anything to protect their territories.

    Specific Instincts of Domesticated Cats

    We all look at our cats and think that they are such lazy creatures. All they do is eat, sleep, play, and repeat.
    As we talked about their natural instincts and wild cat instincts, now we'll talk about some instincts of domesticated cats.

    1. They may start hunting for rats, mice, or lizards in your home as they still have a hunting instinct within them.
    2. They might compete with your other cats for food.
    3. If you have unneutered male cats, they may pee in certain places to mark their scent.
    4. They have an unspecific diet or could be specific, but handling domestic cats are less of a struggle than with wild cats when it comes to food.
