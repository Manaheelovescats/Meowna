    • Behavior

    Cats are adored by everyone, I mean, who doesn't like cats?
    In this world, we have more than 70 different breeds of cats.
    Some of which we can have as housecats and some that are just meant to live out in the wilderness.
    This page will show the common behavior of both male and female cats when it comes to doing different tasks.

    When Playing

    Cats are complex creatures and are one of a kind.
    They are perfect to have as pets and are somehow therapeutic to us humans.
    Now, as joyful and enjoyable as they can be, they are also confusing.
    Here are some ways to tell when a cat wants to play with you or not:

    • They have sudden erratic movements and are unpredictable with their actions.
    • They constantly run around the house as if they're being chased,
      or perhaps act like they're chasing something.
    • Having increased levels of energy. They are probably more joyful and playful
      than they usually are.
    • Often have their eyes focused on an object.
    • Perhaps they'd also start biting your feet as a
      gesture of wanting to play with you.

    When Littering

    Cats are also really clean animals.
    They could also be choosy with their litter boxes.
    As a cat owner, you should know how your cat acts,
    especially when they're trying to use their sandbox.
    Below are actions your cats may have been doing
    when minding their business in their sandbox.

    • You may notice your kitten running into their box, then leaving very quickly right after.
    • You also may notice your little animal releasing its wastes near the box, not inside it.
    • Your cat may also enter their little box to use it but it may hesitate to go in,
      usually because they may have found something unappealing inside.
    • They may have issues with the box itself. Your cat may be scratching surfaces that isn't the litterbox.
      This means they may have issues with their box.
    • Cats may also avoid the box and take longer to potty or pee,
      meaning that the box may be dirty and may have been uncomfortable for them to use.

    When Hungry

    Cats are very lazy and food-obsessed creatures, just like you.
    So, here are some factors your cats may be doing to ask for food:

    • They would excel more and try to play with you. This hints that they want attention and are hungry.
    • They're bored. Some cats just ask for food out of boredom.
    • They're clingy. As we have clingy cats, we also have non-clingy cats.
      If they're not usually clingy, they may cling onto you to show that they are hungry.
    • They could be whining during your mealtime. They'd start crying and meowing nonstop, to hint their hunger.
    • They also may hang around, meowing near where their food is stored.

    When Eating

    If you've noticed, your cats may have certain actions or gestures when eating.
    Here are a few reasons onto why they may be doing this:

    • If you have seen then getting angry at your other cats,
      it means they are keeping the food for themselves, or at least, trying to.
      Cats are usually competitive when it comes to their resources.
    • If you've seen them put their food on the ground, it's because they're hunters.
      They do this act because if they were to hunt, they would be dragging the animal to eat.
    • If they scratch their bowl or plate, it could be because of their instinct to hide their leftovers, like other animals.
    • If your cat is meowing while eating, it may be due to the fact that they're still hungry and
      they aren't getting enough food to eat.
    • If your cat doesn't eat their food yet and still asks for more, it may be caused by their boredom,
      or worse, their lack of calories.
      They may keep whining and crying because they need calories.
